The Big Picture start here

Five number families do most of the heavy lifting on the G280 limits oral. Memorize these scaffolds first; details hang off them.

Master mnemonic — “A S W T F”
Altitudes · Speeds · Weights · Temps · Fluids. Build a number tree in your head with these five trunks. Every limit on the test is a branch.

G280 Altitude Tape — all limits

Hover/tap labels for context. Drawn to scale from MSL → 45,000 ft.

Pattern — APU altitude ladder
15 → 20 → 35 → 40
15k assist start · 20k bleed · 35k start · 40k run.
Gaps: +5, +15, +5. Think “quintuple step, big gap in the middle.”
Pattern — the “20-20-20” rule
Three different limits all live at 20,000 ft: APU bleed, gear extension, flap extension. If you forget any of them, default to 20k and you’ll usually be right.

Speed Tape visual

All KIAS limits on a single horizontal tape so you can see how the flap & gear regimes overlap. Mach limits (0.77 MI) live on a separate band.

G280 Speed Tape — all KIAS limits

From 0 to 280 KIAS. 195 is the big shared number for gear; 250-220-180 is the flap ladder.

Pattern — flap VFE ladder
250 / 220 / 180 for flaps 10° / 20° / LAND.
Drop 30 then 40. (Bigger flap deflection → bigger drop.)
Pattern — the “195 family”
Three gear numbers all land on 195 KIAS: VLO, VLE, and main-tire max ground speed. Nose tire is the odd one out at 182. Emergency extension is the conservative 175, then you can accelerate to 195 once down-and-locked.
Mnemonic — “SAM at 77”
Stab (alternate) trim & Mach trim inop both cap you at 0.77 MI. Two limits, one number.
Mnemonic — “25, the line in the sky”
Above FL250, yaw damper must be on. Below FL250 with yaw damper off, you’re limited to 250 KIAS. Easy pair to remember: 25 and 250.

Avionics

TAWS shall be selected OFF within 15 nm of landing airport when any of:

  • No published instrument approach procedure
  • Longest runway < 3,500 ft
  • Airport not in the database
  • QFE altimeter setting used for approach/landing (subsequent takeoff) without geometric altitude

APPR mode for LOC approaches — select only when within 60° of approach course.

Mnemonic — TAWS OFF, “P.D.Q. S” (Pretty Damn Quick — Short)
Published approach? none → off.  Database? missing → off.  QFE used → off.  Short (<3,500 ft) → off.
All four checks happen inside 15 nm. APPR LOC is 60° — “15 / 60” together.

Pattern — “20 vs 8 = APU helps”
With APU GEN online you’re allowed 2½× more battery charge current at takeoff (20 vs 8). If asked “why?” the answer is just “more available power.”
Pattern — “15 / 3 / 17.5”
Galley & lav outlets are 15A, everything else is 3A, and the total cabin draw caps at 17.5A. Note the cap is barely above one galley outlet — that’s why it’s a hard ceiling.

Imbalance limit doubles from ground (450) to airborne (900). “Twice the limit once you’re in the sky.”

Operational rules

  • Do not operate APU if #2 fire extinguisher has been activated.
  • Approved for unattended operation.
  • Allow 2 min cooldown between APU starts or between shutdown and next start.
  • After 3 consecutive start attempts: 30 min cool down.
  • Operate APU only if right standby fuel pump is operative or right engine is operative.
  • During ground aircraft de-icing: APU OFF, door CLOSED.
Pattern — APU altitudes “15-20-35-40”
15 assist start, 20 bleed, 35 start, 40 run. Two pairs: small at the bottom, big at the top.
Mnemonic — EGT “974 / 718 / 666”
974 Start (under 50%) · 718 Running · 666 Bleeding.
“The hotter the moment, the higher the limit. Steady & sharing air = cooler limits.”
Mnemonic — “2 / 3 / 30”
2-min cooldown between starts · 3 consecutive start attempts max · then 30-min cool. (Same numbers also show up in engine ground rules.)
Pattern — “22 V to spin, 108% never exceed”
Two stand-alone numbers: 22 V battery minimum to start, 108% rotor never-exceed. Both are bookend limits.

Towing angles — 80 / 84 / 94 / 100

A single arc with four trip points: normal range, horn, CAS, and damage.

Mnemonic — towing “80 / 84 / 94 / 100 → NHCD”
Normal 80 · Horn 84 · CAS 94 · Damage 100.
Tiny step (4°) to horn → big step (10°) to CAS → tiny step (6°) to damage. The horn is your “tap on the shoulder.”
Mnemonic — “195 is the gear number”
VLO, VLE, and main-tire max all share 195 KIAS. Only the nose tire (182) and the conservative emerg extension (175) sit lower.
Pattern — brake temps “10 / 100 / 450”
10°C minimum before T/O on contaminated rwy · ~100°C target after taxi to prevent ice · 450°C CAS overheat. Multiplying by ~10 each step.

Ice Protection

  • Wing anti-ice required if icing conditions are imminent or upon detection of ice on wings, winglets, or windshield edges.
  • WAI single-bleed source above 26,000 ft — PROHIBITED.
  • T/O prohibited with frost/ice/snow/slush on wings, stabilizers, control surfaces, engine inlets, or top of fuselage.
  • WAI not recommended above +10°C TAT or below −40°C SAT.
  • Engine anti-ice required:
    • Taxi/T/O when SAT ≤ +10°C
    • Inflight when TAT ≤ +10°C + visible moisture
  • WAI must be ON for T/O if in icing or icing anticipated.
  • Flaps in icing → T/O, approach, landing only. Holding → 0° flaps only.
  • Probe Heat OVRD on ground > 30 sec prohibited.
  • Min holding speed in icing — 185 KIAS.
Mnemonic — “The 10°C rule”
+10°C is the magic ice number: it’s the upper bound for engine anti-ice required and the upper bound where WAI is recommended. Below 10°C → think ice.
Pattern — “SAT on ground, TAT in air”
For engine anti-ice: use SAT when stationary (taxi/T/O), TAT once the temp probe sees ram rise (inflight). Same threshold (10°C), different probe.
Pattern — WAI envelope “−40 to +10”
Don’t use WAI > +10°C TAT (too warm — bleed waste) or < −40°C SAT (too cold — thermal shock risk). A 50°C operating band.

Mnemonic — APR “5 & 10”
5 min takeoff thrust AEO · 10 min APR OEI. “Lose one, get double the time.”
Mnemonic — “Three 30s of starting”
Abort if no lightoff in 30 sec · abort if no oil pressure by 30% N2 · 3 min wait + 40 min dry-crank re-do bracket the 30s.
Mnemonic — “3-15 starter rule”
3 starts → 15 min cool. 5 min motoring → 15 min cool. The cool-down number never changes.
Mnemonic — Oil temp “−40 / +5 / +15 / 138 / 154”
Start can be ice-cold (−40). Past idle you need warmth (+5). Above climb thrust, hotter still (+15). Cruise cap 138, brief transient 154 (max 2 min). Pattern: the harder the engine works, the higher the floor.
Mnemonic — “ITT 650 below 40, 950 above 40”
Below 40% N2 a hot start would mean fuel-rich without airflow → 650°C. Above 40% N2 there’s airflow to cool → 950°C allowed.
Pattern — wind for start vs T/O
Engine start tolerates more wind than T/O: crosswind 20, tailwind 15. T/O tailwind limit is only 10. (Start = on the brakes, more margin.)

Misc volts / amps / psi

  • APU batt start 22 V · rotor 108%

  • Cabin outlets 15A / 3A · total ≤ 17.5A
